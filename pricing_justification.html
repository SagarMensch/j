Executive Positioning

How this pricing should be framed in the room

The proposal should be positioned as a governed operational intelligence platform, not as a generic chatbot subscription. The pricing is justified by the delivery model: plant-specific deployment, role-based governance, multilingual readiness, document-grounded responses, training workflows, and production support.

What client is paying for
Operational reliability
Controlled rollout, governed access, structured document ingestion, and enterprise support.
What should be emphasized
Adoption and risk reduction
Faster SOP access, reduced search time, cleaner auditability, and smoother multi-plant standardization.
What should be avoided
Provider-level details
Do not anchor the conversation on model vendors, vector tools, or low-level infrastructure names.

Why this holds

Core pricing defense

  • The setup fee is justified because the product is not being sold as a generic SaaS seat; it is being rolled out as a tailored multi-plant operating system.
  • The licensing fee is defensible because it includes governed access, auditability, document controls, and platform support, not just chat access.
  • The query pricing is usage-linked and therefore more transparent than opaque unlimited plans that become difficult to defend later.
  • The recurring support structure is moderate and commercially clean at 10 percent of setup, which procurement usually finds easier to accept.
  • The architecture remains portable, which supports the no-lock-in narrative during long-term commercial negotiation.
Recommended commercial line: this is priced as a governed plant-assistant platform with deployment accountability, not as raw model consumption resale.

Licensing

Named user model

The licensing structure is intentionally named-user based rather than concurrent-user based, because plant operations require accountability, audit traceability, and personal activity history.

  • Admin user annual fee: Rs 30,000
  • Standard user annual fee: Rs 21,600
  • Total user count basis: 100
  • Total annual licensing: Rs 25,80,000
Usage

Query pricing logic

Query pricing should be explained as workload-based consumption. Simple SOP lookups and complex cross-document analysis do not consume the same resources, so the structure remains commercially rational.

  • Selected reference bundle: Standard
  • Monthly reference bundle: Rs 12,000
  • Annual query reference: Rs 1,44,000
  • Best framing: transparent, usage-linked, and controllable
Support

Recurring support rationale

The AMC line is deliberately moderate. It supports maintenance, operational adjustments, bug handling, and continuity without reopening the full setup budget each year.

  • AMC basis: 10 percent of setup
  • Annual AMC: Rs 2,40,000
  • Commercial benefit: easy to explain and easy to renew
  • Procurement posture: cleaner than large unspecified support buckets

Negotiation Guardrails

What can move and what should not

  • Can move: payment milestones, query bundle choice, selected add-ons, and cloud-hosting packaging.
  • Can move carefully: Year 1 commercials through phased rollout, limited pilot scoping, or plant-count staging.
  • Should not move aggressively: core setup fee, because that is where delivery effort and deployment accountability sit.
  • Best concession path: keep setup intact, reduce Year 1 pressure through phased user rollout or a smaller initial bundle.
  • Avoid: cutting licensing too early. It weakens long-term pricing discipline and is harder to recover later.
Deployment Smoothness

Commercial points that make rollout easier

  • Use a phased rollout narrative: pilot plants first, then scale across the network.
  • Keep infrastructure described as a delivery baseline, not as a stack shopping list.
  • Lead with multilingual readiness, governed answers, and auditability as adoption drivers.
  • Anchor Year 1 on implementation and operating reliability, not on raw AI cost.
  • If procurement presses on price, respond with scope and sequencing rather than pure discounting.
Cleanest path in discussion: protect the setup fee, keep support logic simple, and use rollout phasing as the primary commercial flexibility lever.
